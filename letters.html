広重の描いた「名所江戸百景」は、場所を特定できるほど具体的です。だから版画をフィールドガイドの様に読んで、その場所を訪れると、描かれた特徴が今も残っていると驚いたり、全く消えていると気づいたり、旅でもしている気分になります。また、遠くまで見渡せた時代の風景版画を眺めた後に、その場所へ自転車でむかうと、関東台地、海、川、と地形まで感じられます。
では、なぜこんなことを楽しんでいるのかと考えると、多分「旅」の準備作業、広重の意図を調べるうちにユーモアある視点に気づいて、毎日の小さな楽しみの捉え方に気づく気がするから、ではないかと思います。オリジナルの出版時期は１８５６年から１８５８年の間で、黒船来航から通商条約を結ぶまでの期間、言い換えるとその後の急激な近代化、価格高騰、国内変化の直前でした。過去のリサーチの中には、出版内容に規制がかかっていた中で、状況を伝える記号を読み解いているものもあり、広重チームが当時の問題や混乱を無視することなく、軽やかに扱う視点に気づきます。
これは、そんな「名所江戸百景」を手に現地を訪れてみる旅の絵日記です。

One Hundred Famous Views of Edo, by Hiroshige is topologically so specific that many of the places are still traceable on a map. I approached each print like a field guide, referencing previous researches along the way. Then I hopped on my bicycle with my sketches to visit the site. Along the way, the physically landscape of the Kanto plateau, the sea, and the rivers all came alive. Visiting the sites, I noticed traces that still remain or became off a past that has vanished.
 I wondered why I find this project fascinating. Maybe because I'm found of its preparation very much that I have conversation with Hiroshige through his prints. When the world feels bit too much, I find myself encouraged by his sense of humorous, that notices joy in the everyday life without ignoring the issues of his time. The series was published between 1856 and 1858 after American navy arrived (1853) Yokohama to request open the port to sign the Treaty of Amenity and Commerce (1858) with the United States that triggered drastic modernization, inflation and domestic conflicts in Japan. Accordingly, some researchers pointed that Hiroshige's publishing team secretly printed their messages on changes in Edo. For example, cherry blossom viewing at Gotenyama which had been caved out for construction of cannon batteries at Odaiba against the foreign force, or Snow covered Asakusa temple in summer to celebrate its post-earthquake restoration with red and white decoration.
 This is an ongoing series of visual letters from my visits.
